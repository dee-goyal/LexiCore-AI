# Sample Constitution Article

## Article 21 - Protection of Life and Personal Liberty

No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law.

### Key Points:
- This is a fundamental right under Part III of the Constitution
- It is one of the most important articles
- Cannot be suspended even during Emergency (except in case of arrest)

### Interpretation:
The Supreme Court has interpreted "life" to mean not merely animal existence but living with human dignity. This includes:
- Right to livelihood
- Right to clean environment
- Right to education
- Right to privacy
- Right to health
- Right to shelter

### Landmark Cases:
1. Maneka Gandhi v. Union of India (1978) - Broadened the scope of Article 21
2. Francis Coralie v. Union Territory of Delhi (1981) - Right to live with human dignity
3. Olga Tellis v. Bombay Municipal Corporation (1985) - Right to livelihood

### Limitations:
- Can be restricted by "procedure established by law"
- Procedure must be fair, just and reasonable
- Law must be valid and constitutional
